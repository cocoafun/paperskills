# Drafting Playbook

Use this reference when converting structured research inputs into paper prose.

## Core sequence

1. Confirm the target artifact.
2. Build or validate the section list.
3. Map claims to evidence before writing.
4. Draft section by section.
5. Mark unsupported areas explicitly.

## Section discipline

- Introduction: problem, stakes, gap, contribution.
- Related work: synthesis and contrast, not bibliography.
- Method: enough detail to understand how claims are supported.
- Results or evaluation: only claims supported by visible evidence.
- Discussion: implications, limits, and future directions.

## Writing rules

- Keep each paragraph tied to one purpose.
- Distinguish prior literature from the paper's own contribution.
- When evidence is partial, use placeholders or limitation notes.
- Do not smooth over open dependencies with confident prose.

## Failure modes

- drafting a polished introduction with no evidence map
- mixing user goals and paper claims
- repeating the same evidence across sections without a clear role
- overstating contribution before method and evidence are established
