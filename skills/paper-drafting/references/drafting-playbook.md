# Drafting Playbook

Use this reference when converting structured research inputs into paper prose.

## Core sequence

1. Confirm the target artifact.
2. Confirm manuscript type and study-completion status.
3. Build or validate the section list.
4. Map claims to evidence before writing.
5. Draft section by section.
6. Mark unsupported areas explicitly.

## Manuscript archetypes

- `literature-review-paper`: synthesis-first, explicit source coverage, no fabricated field results.
- `conceptual-paper`: theory or mechanism-first, framework and propositions are central, recommendations stay secondary.
- `proposal-style-manuscript`: future-tense method and expected contribution, no pretend results section.
- `empirical-paper`: requires visible method, data, and results support.

## Section discipline

- Introduction: problem, stakes, gap, contribution.
- Related work: synthesis and contrast, not bibliography.
- Method: enough detail to understand how claims are supported.
- Results or evaluation: only claims supported by visible evidence.
- Discussion: implications, limits, and future directions.

If the manuscript type is `conceptual-paper` or `proposal-style-manuscript`, a framework or research-design section can replace `Results`, but not the entire scholarly middle of the paper.
If the target artifact is an undergraduate thesis, the working draft should usually include a chapter-complete middle, not only a three-heading article outline.

## Writing rules

- Keep each paragraph tied to one purpose.
- Distinguish prior literature from the paper's own contribution.
- When evidence is partial, use placeholders or limitation notes.
- Do not smooth over open dependencies with confident prose.
- Keep recommendation-oriented prose inside `Discussion`, `Implications`, or `Future Work`; do not let it become the paper's main backbone.

## Failure modes

- drafting a polished introduction with no evidence map
- mixing user goals and paper claims
- repeating the same evidence across sections without a clear role
- overstating contribution before method and evidence are established
- turning the manuscript into an industry report, consulting memo, or policy brief with academic citations attached
- saving only workflow narration such as “hand off to finalization” instead of a real inspectable draft
